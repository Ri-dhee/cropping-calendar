# Bhutan Smart Farmer - Research-Driven Agricultural Calendar

A research-based agricultural planning tool for Bhutan that provides evidence-backed farming calendars with confidence scores, source citations, and expert disclaimers.

## 🌱 Research-Driven Features

### Enhanced Reliability & Accuracy
- **Source Citations**: All recommendations reference official government guidelines, academic research, and traditional knowledge systems
- **Confidence Scoring**: Each recommendation includes confidence levels (0-100%) based on research reliability
- **Climate Zone Integration**: Automatically identifies appropriate agro-ecological zones (Subtropical, Temperate, Alpine)
- **Research Validation**: Cross-references traditional practices with modern scientific methods

### Key Data Sources
- Ministry of Agriculture and Livestock, Bhutan
- Royal University of Bhutan - College of Natural Resources
- National Biodiversity Centre, Bhutan
- FAO Country Profiles and Climate-Smart Agriculture guidelines
- Traditional Bhutanese agricultural knowledge systems

### Reliability Indicators
- **Overall Confidence Score**: Aggregate reliability assessment
- **Monthly Confidence Levels**: Specific confidence for each month's recommendations
- **Climate Zone Identification**: Altitude-based zone classification
- **Source Attribution**: Clear references for each recommendation
- **Uncertainty Acknowledgment**: Transparent about limitations and variability

### Enhanced Output Features
- **Research Information Panel**: Displays climate zone and overall confidence
- **Source References**: Lists all scientific and traditional sources used
- **Monthly Notes**: Context-specific guidance for each month
- **Expert Disclaimers**: Important limitations and consultation advice
- **Visual Confidence Indicators**: Color-coded confidence levels

## 🔬 Scientific Approach

### Agro-Ecological Zone Classification
- **Subtropical (600-2000m)**: Rice, maize, millet, vegetables
- **Temperate (2000-4000m)**: Barley, buckwheat, potato, mustard
- **Alpine (4000m+)**: Yak herding, medicinal plants

### Climate Considerations
- **Monsoon Period (June-September)**: Heavy rainfall impacts and drainage management
- **Winter (December-February)**: Frost protection and cold limitations
- **Dry Season (October-March)**: Irrigation requirements and water management

### Validation Criteria
- Altitude compatibility assessment
- Seasonal timing validation
- Traditional practice integration
- Modern research alignment

## 📈 Usage Guidelines

1. **Input Requirements**: Provide specific location and crop/livestock details
2. **Interpretation**: Review confidence scores and source citations
3. **Local Consultation**: Always consult with local agricultural extension officers
4. **Adaptation**: Adjust recommendations based on specific site conditions
5. **Monitoring**: Track actual results against predictions for continuous improvement

## ⚠️ Important Disclaimers

- Recommendations are based on general regional patterns and may require local adaptation
- Climate change is altering traditional agricultural patterns
- Site-specific factors (soil, microclimate, water) significantly impact success
- Professional consultation with local experts is strongly recommended
- Market conditions and food security needs should influence decisions
